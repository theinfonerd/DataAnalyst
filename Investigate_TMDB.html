Introduction¶

This data set contains information about 10,866 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. Which genres are most popular from year to year? Does a higher budget correspond to higher user rating? What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have high revenues?

Research Question 1: Does spending more money on your film increase the chance of high ratings?¶

Remember that we removed movies that did not list an adjusted budget which reduced the sample size. We're going to narrow in further by looking at plus or minus one standard deviation.

genre_names = ['Action', 'Adventure', 'Animation', 'Comedy', 'Crime', 'Documentary', 'Drama', 'Family', 'Fantasy', 'History', 'Horror', 'Music', 'Mystery', 'Romance', 'Science Fiction', 'Thriller', 'TV Movie']

With the histograms side-by-side, we can see clearly the difference in the number of movies in each genre produced each year. The only genre to stay the same was History. 2014 out-paced the following year with Romance, Drama, Documentary, Comedy, and Action movies while the Horror and Science Fiction genres experienced significant increases in 2015.

Conclusions¶

For question 1, does spending more money on your film increase the chance of high ratings, there appears to be no apparent correlation between the budget and the user rating. For question 2, which genres were popular in 2014 and 2015, we examined a histogram to draw some general conclusions. 2014 had more Romance, Drama, Documentary, Comedy, and Action movies. 2015 had move Horror and Science Fiction movies.
